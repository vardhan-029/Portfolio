Bone Fracture Detection

Bone fractures are the very common injuries which require immediate and correct diagnosis to treat patients effectively. Conventionally, traditional radiographic assessment is usually performed by radiologists, whose accuracy varies largely. Treatment may be delayed for this reason alone. This work discusses the potential of advanced deep learning model YOLOv8 for automated bone fracture detection from medical images. We used a large dataset containing multiple varieties of radiographs with labeled fractures and trained the YOLOv8 model to recognize and classify different types of fractures. The results obtained from our experiments show that the YOLOv8 model is quite accurate, sensitive, and specific for fracture detections. Such results are quite outstanding and comparable with conventional diagnosis techniques. The time needed to make detection for fractures can be significantly reduced, but this will not compromise on a high level of reliability.
